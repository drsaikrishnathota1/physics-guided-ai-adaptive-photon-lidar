Dear Editor,

Please consider our short communication entitled “Physics-Guided Adaptive
Photon Allocation for Defense-Oriented Single-Photon LiDAR” for publication
in Optics & Laser Technology.

This work presents an interpretable adaptive sensing strategy for
photon-limited single-photon LiDAR. The proposed method combines posterior
target uncertainty, predicted optical signal-to-background ratio, and
measurement-count exploration to determine where the next photon allocation
should be directed.

The numerical framework incorporates beam divergence, target-footprint
coupling, two-way atmospheric attenuation, atmospheric backscatter,
time-of-flight histogram formation, and important SPAD detector
nonidealities. The proposed allocation method is evaluated against uniform,
random, greedy-posterior, and entropy-based strategies under an identical
photon budget.

Across 30 simulated episodes per method, the proposed physics-guided
information allocation strategy achieved a detection rate of 93.3%,
compared with 76.7% for uniform allocation. It also reduced average
transmitted photon use by approximately 15.7% relative to uniform sensing.

The manuscript is intentionally framed as a focused simulation-based short
communication. It does not claim experimental deployment, exact
mutual-information optimization, artificial-intelligence training, or
quantum advantage. The limitations and required future experimental and
target-absent validation are stated explicitly.

The manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not
under consideration by another journal. No classified, proprietary,
personal, or clinical data were used.

Thank you for considering this manuscript.
