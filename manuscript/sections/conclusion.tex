\section{Conclusion}

A physics-guided adaptive photon-allocation strategy was developed for defense-oriented single-photon LiDAR. The method combines posterior target uncertainty, predicted optical signal-to-background ratio, and measurement-count exploration. In a reproducible simulation study, the strategy achieved a 93.3\% detection rate and reduced average photon consumption by approximately 15.7\% relative to uniform allocation. The findings support the use of physics-guided sequential sensing for improving photon efficiency in constrained LiDAR reconnaissance. Experimental and target-absent validation remain important next steps.
